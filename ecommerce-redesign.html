E-Commerce Website Redesign (EarthWear)

Sustainable fashion e-commerce website designed for an ethical clothing brand.

UI/UX Figma Web Design E-Commerce

Conceptualisation

This project focuses on redesigning the online experience for a fictional sustainable fashion brand called EarthWear. The aim was to create a modern e-commerce interface that communicates ethical production, environmental care and transparent storytelling.

Many fashion websites feel cluttered and transactional. For EarthWear, the design direction was to make the site feel warm, editorial and human-centred: large lifestyle photography, clear typography and strong sustainability messaging such as “Fashion That Cares” and “Our Commitment”.

Planning

Information Architecture

  • Homepage - hero banner, commitment section, featured collection.
  • Shop page - product grid with filters.
  • Product detail page - sustainability details, materials and sizing.
  • Cart and checkout - clean summary and simple steps to purchase.

Wireframes

  • Sketched layouts for the hero banner, three-column “Our Commitment” tiles, product grids and footer structure.
  • Focused on visual hierarchy: hero → brand story → featured products → supporting links.
  • Defined a mobile-first layout similar to the EarthWear mock-up, then scaled to desktop.

Visual Style Guide

  • Soft neutral background with muted green accents for a natural feel.
  • Serif headings for a premium editorial look, paired with a clean sans-serif body font for readability.
  • Rounded cards and pill-shaped buttons such as “Shop Now” and “View All Products”.
  • Simple line icons for logo mark, cart and menu.

Component Library

  • Navigation bar with EarthWear logo and icons.
  • Reusable product cards (image, name, price, sustainability tags).
  • Sustainability badges displaying percentage scores.
  • Buttons, tags, input fields and footer link lists.

UI Design & Development

Homepage

  • Full-bleed hero image with overlay text: “Fashion That Cares” and a short sustainability statement.
  • Primary call-to-action button “Shop Now” placed centrally for high visibility.
  • “Our Commitment” section explaining the brand values, paired with a “View All Products” button.
  • Featured product row showcasing key pieces with sustainability-themed copy.

Shop Page

  • Product grid layout with consistent cards: image, product name, short description and price.
  • Sustainability labels showing a percentage score to communicate impact at a glance.
  • Left-hand filters for category and price to improve browsing efficiency.

Product Detail Page

  • Large product image on the left, information panel on the right.
  • Breakdown of materials (for example, recycled cotton and organic fibres) and sustainability score.
  • Size selector, quantity stepper and clear “Add to Cart” button.
  • Additional sections for care instructions, shipping and returns to build trust.

Cart & Checkout

  • Clean cart summary with product thumbnails, quantity controls and line totals.
  • Order summary panel showing subtotal, shipping and final total before payment.
  • Simple checkout form broken into shipping details and payment information to reduce cognitive load.

Reflection & Learning

This project helped me practise designing a complete e-commerce journey, from storytelling on the homepage to the final checkout. I learnt how consistent typography, spacing and colour choices can make a sustainable brand feel both trustworthy and aspirational.

I also improved my ability to design with components in Figma, ensuring that product cards, buttons and navigation remain consistent across multiple pages. In the future, I would like to extend the prototype with responsive breakpoints and simple usability testing to validate the layout with real users.
